Digestive System

The digestive system is responsible for breaking down food into nutrients, absorbing them into the bloodstream, and removing waste from the body.

Main Functions

  • Digestion of food
  • Absorption of nutrients
  • Elimination of waste

Main Organs

  • Mouth: Starts digestion by chewing food
  • Esophagus: Moves food to stomach
  • Stomach: Breaks food using acids
  • Small Intestine: Absorbs nutrients
  • Large Intestine: Absorbs water

Digestion Process

  1. Food enters mouth
  2. Moves through esophagus
  3. Digested in stomach
  4. Absorbed in intestines
  5. Waste removed

Common Diseases

  • Acid reflux
  • Ulcers
  • Irritable bowel syndrome

Interesting Facts

  • The small intestine is about 6 meters long
  • Digestion can take up to 24 hours
